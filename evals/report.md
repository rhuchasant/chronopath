## Methodology

10 prompts × 4 personas, each generated by N Claude models (Sonnet 4.5, Opus 4.5, Haiku 4.5), scored by a judge across four dimensions:

1. **Factual accuracy** — every claim traces to a provided source
2. **Persona fit** — narrative matches the persona's prompting directive
3. **Cultural sensitivity** — no orientalism, condescension, or flattening
4. **Source-bias awareness** — uses sources critically, not interchangeably

**Cross-model judging within Claude family:** when generating with Sonnet, the judge is Haiku (or Opus); when generating with Haiku, the judge is Sonnet. The judge model is never the same as the generator. This reduces — but does not eliminate — self-preference bias, since all judges share the same model family.

## Limitations (stated openly)

- LLM-as-judge is useful for *relative* model ranking, not absolute scoring
- All judges are Claude family models — cross-family validation (e.g., adding GPT-4 or Gemini as a third judge) is future work
- Judges inherit biases toward verbose, hedged outputs
- Spot-checked by hand on a sample (see `/evals/spot-checks/` for human–LLM agreement notes)
- Corpus is small (~30 sources, 5 stops), so absolute scores are not stable across runs
